The Problem
Scale

Over 500,000 instances of hate speech are posted online every day. Manual moderation cannot keep pace with the volume, leaving platforms reactive rather than preventive.

Nuance

Hate speech evolves constantly. Coded language, dog whistles, and context-dependent slurs evade keyword filters. Detection requires deep contextual understanding, not pattern matching.

Consequence

Unmoderated hate speech radicalises individuals, silences vulnerable communities, and erodes democratic discourse. The cost of inaction is measured in real human harm.

The Pipeline
01

Ingest

Content is ingested in real-time from platform APIs, webhooks, or batch uploads. All text is normalised and stripped of formatting artefacts.

02

Analyse

A multi-layer NLP pipeline examines linguistic features, contextual signals, and semantic intent. The model distinguishes genuine threats from protected speech.

03

Classify

Each piece of content is assigned a severity tier from 1 to 5, along with category labels and confidence scores. Ambiguous cases are flagged for human review.

04

Route

Classified content is routed to the appropriate response queue. Tier 5 triggers immediate escalation. Lower tiers follow configurable policy workflows.

05

Report

Comprehensive analytics are generated per cycle. Trend data, false-positive rates, and demographic impact assessments feed back into model refinement.

Severity Tiers
Tier Classification Description Response
Tier 1 Neutral No hateful content detected No action required
