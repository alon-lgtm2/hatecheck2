Key Capabilities

A comprehensive suite of tools for monitoring, classifying, and reporting antisemitic content across digital platforms.

Real-Time Content Monitoring

Continuous scanning of social media platforms, news outlets, and online forums to detect antisemitic content as it emerges across the European digital landscape.

Multi-Framework Classification

Automated assessment against IHRA, Nexus, JDA, and Dutch Penal Code definitions, enabling nuanced legal and policy-relevant categorisation of flagged content.

Trend Analysis & Reporting

Statistical dashboards and periodic intelligence reports tracking the prevalence and evolution of antisemitic narratives, with exportable data for institutional use.

Cross-Border Intelligence Sharing

Secure API integrations enabling European agencies and civil society partners to exchange flagged content data in compliance with GDPR and fundamental rights standards.

Grounded in International Standards

Every classification is assessed against established legal and scholarly definitions of antisemitism.

IHRA
International Holocaust Remembrance Alliance
Working Definition of Antisemitism (2016). Adopted by 43 member countries and the European Parliament.
Nexus
Nexus Document Task Force
The Nexus Document (2021). Distinguishing antisemitism from legitimate criticism of Israeli policy.
JDA
Jerusalem Declaration on Antisemitism
Jerusalem Declaration (2021). A scholarly supplement to the IHRA definition with contextual guidelines.
Sr 137c–e
Dutch Penal Code
Articles 137c–137e Wetboek van Strafrecht. Criminal provisions on group insult, incitement, and discrimination.
