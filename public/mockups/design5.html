Understanding the problem

The Challenge

Hate speech monitoring today is fragmented, inconsistent, and often too slow. Communities deserve better.

Fragmented reporting

Incidents are reported through dozens of disconnected channels, making it impossible to see the full picture or respond quickly.

Subjective classification

Without a structured framework, incident severity varies wildly between observers, undermining consistency and trust.

Delayed response

Without real-time structured data, response teams lack the situational awareness to intervene when it matters most.

Community disconnect

Affected communities rarely see the outcomes of their reports, eroding trust in the systems meant to protect them.

Classification framework

Severity Tier System

A structured five-tier system ensuring proportionate, consistent response to every incident.

01
Concerning Speech
Biased language or microaggressions that don't directly target individuals but contribute to a hostile environment over time.
02
Targeted Hostility
Deliberate derogatory language aimed at specific groups or individuals based on protected characteristics.
03
Coordinated Harassment
Organised campaigns of hate involving multiple actors or sustained targeting across platforms and communities.
04
Incitement
Language that explicitly calls for discrimination, exclusion, or violence against protected groups.
05
Imminent Threat
Direct, credible threats of physical violence or actions that pose immediate danger to individuals or communities.
